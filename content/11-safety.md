# Safety & emergencies

In an emergency, your safety comes first — call the emergency number, then let the host know when you can.

!!! note "Security cameras"
    For your safety and security, there are **exterior cameras** at the **entryway** and the **patio**. There are **no cameras inside the house** — your privacy indoors is fully respected.

- **Emergency services:** dial **911**.
- **Nearest ER:** Hoag Hospital, Newport Beach — 1 Hoag Dr (open 24 hours, ~2 mi).
- **Urgent care (non-emergency):** Exer Urgent Care, 235 E 17th St, Costa Mesa — open 8am–8pm.
- **Electrical panel / breakers:** on the **east side of the house, by the garbage**.
- **Water shut-off valve:** **east side of the house**, in the garbage section — turn clockwise to stop the water.
- **Gas shut-off:** on the **west side of the house, behind the fireplace** (reachable from the backyard only) — look for the **red handle**.
- **Hot tub power shut-off:** on the wall near the Lion grill.
- **Fire extinguishers:** throughout the house — see the panel below.
- **First-aid kit:** [location].
- **Smoke & carbon-monoxide alarms:** a **CO alarm** in the **downstairs hallway**, and **smoke alarms in every bedroom** — if one chirps, it's usually the battery; let us know.
- **Two ways out:** in a hurry, the **front door** and the **patio door at the back**.

!!! warning "Fire extinguishers — know where they are"
    There are **eight fire extinguishers** throughout the house. Please familiarise yourself with the locations, and make sure everyone in your group — children included — knows where they are and how to use one.

    - **Upstairs:** the kids' bathroom and the master bathroom — both **under the sink**.
    - **Downstairs:** under the **bathroom sink**, the **laundry sink**, in the **pantry**, under the **bar sink**, and under the **kitchen sink**.
    - **Outdoors:** **under the Big Green Egg**.

    The one **under the kitchen sink is a special kitchen extinguisher** — use that one for kitchen or grease fires.

!!! danger "If you smell gas"
    Don't switch anything electrical on or off. Open the windows, leave the house, and call the emergency number from outside.

!!! warning "Fire & carbon monoxide"
    With gas appliances and several fireplaces here, a few essentials: carbon monoxide is invisible and odourless, so never block the smoke or CO alarms, and **never bring the fire pit, tabletop fireplace, a BBQ, or any open flame indoors or into an enclosed space**. Don't leave the gas range or a lit fireplace unattended, and keep anything flammable well clear of the flames.

!!! note "If there's a fire"
    Get everyone out first, then call **911** from outside — don't go back in for belongings. Only use the extinguisher on a small, contained fire that hasn't started to spread.

!!! note "Earthquakes"
    Southern California gets the occasional tremor. If the ground shakes, **Drop, Cover, and Hold On** under a sturdy table, away from windows and the fireplaces. Afterwards, check for the smell of gas and shut it off if you notice any.

!!! note "Travelling with little ones"
    Please keep children supervised near the **open fire pit** and the **stairs**, store the **gas keys out of reach**, and take care with **water around the upstairs tub** (see the quirks).
